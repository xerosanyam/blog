Financial Management for Youth

Apr 01, 2015

This is what I learnt during a lecture series in my College.
Before making an investment, check for the following properties in the thing you are investing:
Liquidity: How easy it is to access your money.
Risk: How safe your money is.
Return: What profit investment will result in.

Liquidity, Risk and Return are interrelated:

  • Liquidity [math]\propto[/math] 1/Return
  • Risk [math]\propto[/math] Return

Places to invest

  1. Cash: This constitutes of Savings Account, Current Account, etc. (Least Risk, Least Reward)
  2. Bond(Like Loan; I owe you): Indian Government Bond, Corporate Bonds. (Moderate Risk, Moderate Reward)

  3. Stock: NASDAQ, NSE. (Most Risk, Most Reward)

      • *Stock:
  • Valuation:
    • Cash Dividend
    • Share AppreciationMost tech companies do not offer cash dividend. One of its reason could be because income tax is to be paid on dividend.]
      When Cash dividend is given to stockholders, it is announced earlier. and it is not that people buy stock at that time because the price decreases.

  • Types of Stocks

    • Foreign Market: US, Europe, Australia
    • Emerging Market: BRIC (Brazil, Russia, India, China)

    • Commodities: Oil, Natural Resource

      * * *Investment Ideas for a Youth
  1. Set up an emergency fund to cover at least 6 months of your living expense.
  2. Save at least 10% of your net pay(after deductions of tax, etc), beginning with your first paycheck.
  3. Now, suppose you are left with x amount money, out of x, keep at least 20% of it in cash form, rest you can use for Stock market and Bonds.
  4. Now out of, 0.8x, invest in stock market and bond by your age rule. If your age is y; invest (100-y)% of 0.8x in Stock Market and Rest in Bond.
  5. Stock Market Tips:
    • Buy Low, Sell High
    • Invest only fixed amount, at regular interval.
    • Don't pay attention daily.
